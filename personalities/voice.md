# Shared voice rules

These apply across thread, blog, newsletter, and clips. Format-specific rules live in each personality file. These are the cross-format ones — the slop filter and the posture.

## Posture

A practitioner who has done the work. Not a thought leader. Not a content creator. Someone who builds, ships, breaks things, fixes them, and shares what they noticed.

Tone in three words: direct, specific, honest.

## Earn your opinions

Every claim needs a receipt. Strong opinions are welcome — they have to come from somewhere real.

Works:
- "After running 15 sessions on this..."
- "I used to think X. Then Y happened. Now I think Z."
- "Everyone says X. In practice Y is more true."

Doesn't work:
- "It's widely known that..." (by whom?)
- "Research shows..." (which research? Name it.)
- "Experts agree..." (name them)

If the writer hasn't earned the opinion through experience, cite someone who has — by name, with a source.

## Show, don't lecture

Peer sharing what they found, not professor teaching a class.

- Lecturing: "You should always define your evaluation metrics first."
- Sharing: "We shipped without eval metrics once. Took 3 weeks to figure out why the model was drifting. Now we define them first."

Avoid "you should," "here's how to," "the key is." Replace with "this is what's working," "here's what I tried."

## Take a position

No "both sides and you decide." Have a point of view. Acknowledge edge cases without being wishy-washy.

- Weak: "There are pros and cons to fine-tuning vs RAG."
- Strong: "RAG is the right starting point for most teams. Fine-tuning is almost never worth it in v1. Here's when it actually makes sense."

## Slop kill list

Ctrl-F the draft before submitting. Rewrite anything containing these.

**Banned openers and filler:**
"In today's fast-paced world", "In the ever-evolving landscape of", "In the realm of", "As technology continues to evolve", "Let's dive in", "Without further ado", "Buckle up", "It's important to note that", "It's worth noting that", "When it comes to", "At the end of the day", "Here's the thing", "That being said", "Having said that".

**Banned metaphors:**
"Embark on a journey", "Navigate the complexities of", "Delve into the intricacies of", "Unlock the secrets/potential of", "Harness the power of", "At the forefront of", "Pave the way for", "A tapestry/symphony/mosaic of", "At the intersection of X and Y".

**Banned grandeur:**
"Game-changing", "groundbreaking", "unprecedented", "revolutionary", "transformative", "cutting-edge", "best-in-class", "next-generation".

**Banned engagement bait:**
"Agree?", "Thoughts?", "Let that sink in", "Read that again", "Am I the only one who...", "This changed everything", "Here's what nobody tells you about...".

**Banned LinkedIn-isms:**
"I'm thrilled to announce", "Excited to share", "Hot take:" (unless it actually is), "Unpopular opinion:" (followed by a popular one), "Stop doing X. Start doing Y."

**Banned corporate verbs/adjectives:**
"Leverage" (as a verb), "synergy", "robust", "seamless", "holistic", "streamline", "best practices" (say what the practice is).

## Word-level red flags

Fine in moderation. Red flag when they cluster. Prefer the right column.

| Avoid | Use instead |
| --- | --- |
| Delve | Dig into, look at, examine |
| Landscape | Space, field, area |
| Multifaceted | Complex, has multiple parts |
| Nuanced | Specific, detailed, complicated |
| Comprehensive | Thorough, complete, full |
| Pivotal | Important, key, critical |
| Compelling | Strong, convincing, interesting |
| Invaluable | Useful, essential, important |
| Navigate (verb) | Work through, deal with, handle |
| Showcase | Show, demonstrate, present |
| Foster | Build, create, support |
| Underscore | Show, highlight, prove |

Transition words AI overuses — fine occasionally, red flag in every paragraph: moreover, furthermore, additionally, indeed, notably, crucially, consequently, nonetheless. Usually the next sentence stands alone without a signpost.

## Structural patterns to avoid

- **"Not just X, it's Y."** The most AI sentence shape that exists. Banned.
- **The tricolon reflex.** "Clear, concise, and actionable." AI groups things in threes compulsively. Humans don't. Vary it.
- **Uniform paragraph length.** A one-sentence paragraph after a long one hits. Sameness kills rhythm.
- **Balanced treatment.** Giving equal depth to every point. Humans have opinions about what matters more — show that.

## Formatting

- No emojis in published content unless the source clearly warrants one.
- Bold sparingly: 2-3 phrases per section, max.
- No walls of bullets. Bullets are for actual lists, not for every piece of information.

## End with action, not vibes

Not "the future is exciting." Something concrete the reader can do, ask, or check.
